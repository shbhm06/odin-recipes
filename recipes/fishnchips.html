FISH N CHIPS

Description

Fish and Chips is a classic British dish consisting of battered and deep-fried white fish served with thick-cut fried potatoes. It’s crispy, comforting, and often enjoyed with tartar sauce, mushy peas, or a splash of vinegar.

Ingredients:

  • 500g white fish fillets (like cod or haddock)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 cup cold sparkling water or beer
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 tsp paprika (optional)
  • 3–4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into thick chips
  • Oil for deep frying (vegetable or sunflower oil)
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • Tartar sauce or vinegar (optional)

Steps:

  1. Peel and cut the potatoes into thick chips, soak them in cold water for 30 minutes, then pat dry.
  2. Heat oil in a deep pan or fryer to 160°C (320°F) and fry the chips until soft but not browned, then remove and set aside.
  3. Mix flour, baking powder, salt, pepper, and paprika in a bowl, then whisk in cold sparkling water or beer to form a smooth batter.
  4. Pat fish fillets dry, season lightly with salt and pepper, then dust with flour.
  5. Dip the fish into the batter, let excess drip off, and deep fry at 180°C (350°F) until golden and crisp.
  6. Increase oil temperature and re-fry the chips until golden and crispy.
  7. Drain fish and chips on paper towels, then serve hot with lemon wedges and optional sauces.
